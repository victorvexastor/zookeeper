# Original Research of These Books

*The OMXUS paper suite. Available at omxus.com/research*

---

## Why This Matters

These books rest on arguments that are hard to prove empirically — you cannot randomly assign humans to societies or run RCTs on enclosure design. The language proof provides something indisputable: the same genome yields completely different behavioural output depending on environment. Capacity is shared; expression is installed by context. The burden of proof then shifts to those who claim fixity. The papers below formalise and extend that argument.

## The Paper Suite

### 1. Geographic Birthplace as a Predictor of Primary Language
**File:** `language_acquisition_study.tex`

Rigorous statistical analysis (N = 1.8 billion, mean Cohen's h ≈ 0.93) demonstrating that people speak the language of wherever they grew up.

**The move:** Everyone already accepts this for language. Nobody says "but genetics" for which language you speak. The paper uses that uncontroversial case as the wedge: if the most complex learned behaviour we have is environmentally determined, what makes aggression, religion, values different? The empirical result is undisputed; the implication — that biology does not dictate behavioural destiny — is radical.

**Rigor:** Rigorous statistics. The finding is undisputed; the argument it enables is the point.

### 2. The Environmental Determination of Complex Behaviour
**File:** `environmental_determination.tex`

Extends the language argument to three domains:
- **Cultural-emotional expression** — Plate smashing as celebration (Greek) vs. domestic violence (Anglo)
- **Religious affiliation** — ~80% retention parallels language concordance (72–97%)
- **AI cultural bias** — LLMs trained on English think in English cognitive frames; zero genetics, zero "choice"

**Key insight:** If cultural bias can be installed in a system with zero genetics and zero rational choice, environmental acquisition doesn't require either. It just requires exposure.

**Rigor:** Plausible argument; effect sizes comparable across domains.

### 3. Consensus, Distillation, and Trust
**File:** `consensus_distillation_trust.tex`

Three parts:
- **Part I: Bitcoin** — Rigorous math (Poisson processes, double-spend probability, martingales)
- **Part II: Knowledge Distillation** — Formal analogy between neural network distillation and human behavioural learning. Responsibility and learned behaviour are compatible: you can only retrain a model because it owns its current weights.
- **Part III: Social Consensus** — OMXUS web-of-trust for vote counting; theorems vs. security arguments clearly distinguished.

**Rigor:** Part I rigorous; Part II suggestive (structural correspondence); Part III honest bounds under stated assumptions.

### 4. Mathematical Foundations of Decentralized Trust (v2)
**File:** `mathematical_foundations_v2.tex`

Bitcoin's proven security extended to OMXUS. Explicit rigor labeling: Theorems (proven) vs. Security Bounds (under assumptions) vs. Heuristics vs. Conjectures vs. Open Questions.

**Strongest result:** Theorem 5.1 — OMXUS epoch roots anchored to Bitcoin inherit Bitcoin's reversion probability. The only fully rigorous result for OMXUS.

**Key numbers:** Australian emergency data ≈ 1 in 4,600 people per day require emergency response. In 100,000 verified humans: ~20–50 emergencies/day. The constraint is willingness, not density.

**Rigor:** Theorems for anchoring; honest bounds for social layer.

### 5. Civic Proximity Response: Evidence Synthesis for Wearable-Initiated Community Emergency Networks
**File:** `emergency_response_evidence.tex`

Four stages: (1) structural latency of centralised dispatch (7–14 min median); (2) evidence from GoodSAM, PulsePoint, Hatzalah — civilians respond when alerted; (3) spatial coverage model with willingness discount; (4) how NFC ring + mesh removes barriers of app-based systems.

**Explicit limits:** Cannot prove responder willingness curves, false alarm fatigue, or coverage under heterogeneous adoption. Pilot deployment required.

**Rigor:** Evidence synthesis. Pilot study justification.

### 6. Ethics Submission: Pilot Study of Wearable-Initiated Community Emergency Networks
**File:** `ethics_submission_pilot_study.tex`

HREC application for suburban Australian pilot. NFC ring + app; 60-second community response alongside Triple Zero. 12 months, ~500 participants. Single-arm with historical control (randomisation would degrade mesh density). Independent DSMB, pre-registered analysis, open data, negative-results commitment.

**Rigor:** Study design. Not yet conducted.

### 7. The Ideological Rorschach Test
**File:** `ideological_rorschach.tex`

OMXUS was recognised positively by both Claude (Western/liberal) and DeepSeek (Chinese/collective) — each from within their own framework. Capitalists see decentralisation and markets; communists see collective benefit and liberation from corporate/state control.

**Key insight:** "Who controls identity and resources?" — "Nobody and everybody." An answer neither tradition anticipated. Design by wound vs. design by ideology.

**Rigor:** Observation. Cross-ideological legibility claim.
